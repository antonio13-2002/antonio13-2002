## My story
My interest in cybersecurity started with a simple curiosity: I wanted to understand how things work under the hood. Rather than just learning how to use software, my university studies pushed me to look deeper into how systems are built, where their weaknesses lie, and how they can be protected.

Currently, I am in the final stages of my **Master's Degree in Cybersecurity at Politecnico di Torino**, one of Italy's leading technical universities. My academic path has been intensely focused on moving past high-level abstractions to get my hands dirty with the core mechanics of operating systems, memory management, and network architectures.

I thrive on technical challenges that require a deep dive into source code and system internals. Whether it's analyzing memory management flaws or dissecting complex network protocols, I enjoy the process of identifying vulnerabilities and engineering robust, scalable solutions to mitigate them.
My projects and studies span **low-level analysis**, **network security**, and **OS internals**. My main objective is to develop a rigorous security mindset and to learn the fundamental mechanics of vulnerabilities rather than relying on automated scanners, to translate my academic background into real-world defensive strategies.
